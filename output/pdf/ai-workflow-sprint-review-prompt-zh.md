## Rubric
# Course Planning Review Rubric

Use this rubric to review landing pages, course outlines, curriculum briefs, launch notes, or sales material for a paid or free learning product.

## Scoring

Score each category from 0 to 5.

- 0: missing or unusable
- 1: present but vague
- 2: understandable but incomplete
- 3: workable for an early draft
- 4: strong, specific, and ready to refine
- 5: excellent and launch-ready

## Categories

### 1. Audience Fit

- Names a concrete learner segment.
- Describes the learner's current state, constraints, and motivation.
- Avoids generic audience labels such as beginners, creators, or founders without further detail.

### 2. Pain And Urgency

- States the painful problem in the learner's language.
- Explains why solving it now matters.
- Connects the pain to business, career, creative, or personal outcomes.

### 3. Promise And Outcome

- Makes a believable transformation promise.
- Separates learning outcomes from content topics.
- Shows what the learner will be able to do after completion.

### 4. Curriculum Logic

- Has a clear progression from foundation to application.
- Each module has a job and avoids overlap.
- Exercises, projects, or checkpoints prove skill acquisition.

### 5. Differentiation

- Explains why this course should exist now.
- Shows a distinct perspective, method, framework, or proof source.
- Avoids copying the shape of common competing courses.

### 6. Offer And CTA

- Gives a clear next action.
- Reduces decision friction with format, duration, prerequisites, price or access details.
- CTA text matches learner readiness and course maturity.

## Report Expectations

- Identify the highest-leverage rewrite first.
- Include concrete examples of stronger positioning copy.
- Flag missing assumptions that the course owner must validate before launch.
