# Brand Guidelines Review Rubric

Use this rubric to review text-based brand guideline documents, brand asset pages, course label rules, naming guides, logo usage notes, or design handoff material. This MVP reviews structure and written guidance only; it does not inspect pixels or image files.

## Scoring

Score each category from 0 to 5.

- 0: missing or unusable
- 1: present but unsafe or confusing
- 2: partially useful but inconsistent
- 3: understandable for internal use
- 4: strong enough for repeated production
- 5: production-grade and hard to misuse

## Categories

### 1. Identity Clarity

- Names the brand, product, program, or course family clearly.
- Explains the role of each asset or label.
- Makes the hierarchy between master brand, sub-brand, product, course, and campaign obvious.

### 2. Usage Boundaries

- States what is allowed and what is not allowed.
- Covers spacing, sizing, placement, background, contrast, and lockup rules when relevant.
- Uses concrete examples rather than abstract warnings.

### 3. Label And Naming Consistency

- Defines exact label text, capitalization, punctuation, and variants.
- Prevents accidental drift across slides, websites, documents, and social assets.
- Distinguishes required copy from optional descriptive copy.

### 4. Production Readiness

- Gives enough detail for another designer, marketer, or AI assistant to reproduce the asset correctly.
- Includes file naming, formats, export sizes, or implementation notes when needed.
- Avoids hidden dependencies on private knowledge.

### 5. Accessibility And Legibility

- Mentions minimum contrast, minimum size, readable background use, or typography constraints.
- Flags cases where decorative treatment may hurt comprehension.
- Separates brand expression from content clarity.

### 6. Governance

- Identifies owner, review path, version, or last-updated date.
- Explains how new variants should be proposed.
- Keeps strict rules strict and recommendations clearly optional.

## Report Expectations

- Prioritize mistakes that could create off-brand or unusable assets.
- Suggest exact wording for missing rules.
- Explicitly state when a visual inspection is needed beyond this text-only review.
