# Document Clarity Review Rubric

Use this rubric to review general project briefs, proposals, strategy memos, requirements documents, product pages, internal docs, or handoff notes.

## Scoring

Score each category from 0 to 5.

- 0: missing or unreadable
- 1: vague and hard to act on
- 2: understandable but incomplete
- 3: workable with follow-up
- 4: clear and mostly decision-ready
- 5: crisp, complete, and immediately actionable

## Categories

### 1. Purpose

- States why the document exists.
- Makes the intended decision, action, or alignment outcome clear.
- Separates context from recommendation.

### 2. Audience And Use Case

- Names who the document is for.
- Anticipates what the reader needs to do next.
- Avoids assuming private context.

### 3. Structure And Scanability

- Uses headings that describe the content below them.
- Presents the most important information early.
- Makes status, owners, deadlines, risks, and next actions easy to find.

### 4. Specificity

- Replaces broad claims with concrete facts, examples, constraints, or tradeoffs.
- Defines ambiguous terms.
- Includes enough detail to reduce back-and-forth.

### 5. Decision Readiness

- Identifies open questions and unresolved risks.
- Clarifies recommendation, options, or acceptance criteria.
- Makes blockers visible.

### 6. Actionability

- Ends with owners, next steps, CTA, or requested feedback.
- Uses direct language.
- Avoids leaving the reader with only background information.

## Report Expectations

- Call out the single biggest clarity blocker first.
- Suggest a better outline when the current structure is weak.
- Include a short rewrite for the most important paragraph or CTA.
